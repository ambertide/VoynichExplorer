Pages of the Manuscript

Voynich Manuscript is divided into five general chapters, these seem to contain information about botany, pharmacology, astronomy, cosmology and "recipes", analysis of the text shows that, for instance, words that appear in the botany section tend to also appear in the pharmacology section, lending credence to the idea that the manuscript is natural language.

Furthermore, we know that the Manuscript has been written by more than one person, one of the first analysts of the text, Currier have divided the text into two "languages", Voynich A and Voynich B. this division also has further divisions, Voynich A is written by another writer, and Voynich B seems to be written by four other writers respectively.

Furthermore, some folios are multi-page, and some are missing, these missing folios are the ones with empty text and a black background.
